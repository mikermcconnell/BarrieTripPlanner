## Route Network (Post-New Network Redesign)

### Network Structure
Hybrid hub-and-spoke with some crosstown routes, organized into three tiers:

| Tier | Routes | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Express** | Route 400 | High-speed corridor via Highway 400; Park Place to Georgian College/RVH; 30-min frequency |
| **Frequent/Local** | 2, 7, 8, 10, 11, 12, 100, 101 | Core fixed-route network; most at 30-min frequency; some at 35-min headways for reliability |
| **Transit ON Demand** | 7 zones (A-F, H) | Microtransit in lower-density areas |

### Hubs & Key Locations
- **Primary Hub**: Barrie Allandale Transit Terminal (opened September 21, 2025) — beside Allandale Waterfront GO Station
- **Secondary**: Downtown Mini Hub
- **Northern Transfer Point**: Park Place

### Key Corridors
- Bayfield Street (north-south spine)
- Highway 400 (Express Route 400)
- Dunlop Street (downtown east-west)
- Essa Road / Yonge Street (Route 8 corridor)

- **2 New Flyer XE40** battery-electric buses entered revenue service summer 2025
- 12-month pilot (summer 2025 through summer 2026)
- Total cost: $3.35M ($1.65M federal Zero Emission Transit Fund + $1.7M city)
- Specs: 520 kWh battery, ~408 km range
- Chargers by PowerON (Ontario Power Generation subsidiary), installed February 2024

- Council endorsed full fleet conversion to BEB on June 28, 2021
- Long-term target: Net-zero corporate emissions by 2050

## The New Network (Major Redesign)

### Timeline
- 2021: Barrie Transit Vision consultation initiated
- June 21, 2023: Council approved New Network implementation
- June 2, 2024: **Phase 1 launched** — Routes 1, 3, 4, 5, 6, 100 changed; Express/Frequent/Local tiers introduced; Route 400 added; ToD expanded to 5 zones
- December 4, 2024: Council approved revised Phase 2 (based on rider feedback)
- March 9, 2025: **Phase 2 starts** — Route 2 extended to Pringle/Sproule; Zone G discontinued
- June 8, 2025: Three new ToD zones in south Barrie
- September 21, 2025: Barrie Allandale Transit Terminal opens

### Results
- 14% ridership growth post-redesign
- Up to 25% improvement in on-time performance
- 2024 ridership hit ~4 million (all-time record, 21% above pre-COVID)
- Designed to be cost-neutral

### Consultant
- Dillon Consulting (Dennis Kar as lead presenter)
- Transify used for schedule optimization and runtime analysis

## Instructions for Claude

When assisting Mike with transit planning work:

### Document Drafting
- For **Council reports**: Use formal municipal tone, structured with recommendations, financial implications, and strategic alignment sections. Follow Ontario municipal report standards.
- For **public-facing materials**: Use clear, accessible plain language.
- For **internal analysis**: Use precise, data-driven technical language.
- Always present **options and alternatives** with trade-offs rather than single recommendations.
- Provide **detailed first drafts** that Mike can edit down.

### Data & Analysis
- Ground all recommendations in **data and evidence**.
- Reference relevant **CUTA guidelines** and peer comparisons (Guelph, Kingston, Thunder Bay).
- Use the KPIs Mike tracks: ridership, revenue, service hours, coverage, on-time performance.
- When analyzing trends, always consider the **pre-COVID baseline (2019: 3.3M riders)** vs current performance (~4M in 2024).

### Context Awareness
- Barrie Transit is **contract-operated by MVT Canadian Bus** — Mike is on the city (client) side, not the operator side.
- The **New Network redesign** launched in 2024 is a defining recent change — reference it when relevant.
- The **2027-2032 Strategic Plan** is the current primary project.
- Budget context: ~$27.2M operating, ~$17.5M net taxpayer cost — fiscal responsibility matters.
- Growth context: Barrie is one of Ontario's fastest-growing cities — service must keep pace.

### Benchmarking
- Primary peers: **Guelph Transit, Kingston Transit, Thunder Bay Transit**
- Use **CUTA small urban** peer group data when available.
- Consider other Ontario municipalities of similar size (100,000-200,000 population) as secondary comparators.

### When Uncertain
- Ask clarifying questions rather than assuming.
- Flag when data may be outdated and suggest verification.
- Note when recommendations would benefit from operator (MVT) input or data.
